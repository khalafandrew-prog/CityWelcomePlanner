About London

London is the capital of England and the United Kingdom. The city is filled with historic landmarks, world-class museums, beautiful parks and amazing food from all over the world.

Key City Info

  • Currency: British Pound Sterling (£)
  • Country code: +44 (0)
  • Time zone: GMT / BST in summer
  • Plugs: Type G, 3-pin
  • Transport: TfL – Underground, Overground, DLR, buses, trains
  • Payment: Contactless card, phone pay, or Oyster

Attractions & Landmarks

Classic London sights to add to your CityWP plan.

  • Big Ben & Houses of Parliament – Westminster, Westminster station
  • Tower Bridge & Tower of London – Tower Hill station
  • London Eye – Views of the Thames & skyline, Waterloo station
  • Buckingham Palace – Royal residence, Green Park / Victoria stations
  • Trafalgar Square & National Gallery – Charing Cross station
  • Sky Garden – Free views (book ahead), Monument station

Museums

Many London museums are free to enter (special exhibitions may charge).

  • British Museum – World history & culture, Holborn / Tottenham Court Road
  • Natural History Museum – Dinosaurs & nature, South Kensington
  • Science Museum – Hands-on science & tech, South Kensington
  • Victoria & Albert Museum – Art & design, South Kensington
  • Tate Modern – Modern art in a former power station, Blackfriars / Southwark

Family & Kids

Ideas that work well with children and families.

  • Natural History Museum – dinosaurs, fossils, free entry
  • Science Museum – interactive galleries & IMAX (some paid)
  • London Zoo – Regent’s Park, Camden Town / Regent’s Park stations
  • Diana Memorial Playground – Pirate-ship playground in Kensington Gardens
  • London Eye – gentle 30-minute ride with city views

Parks & Gardens

Free green spaces to relax, picnic and walk.

  • Hyde Park & Kensington Gardens – central, lakes, playgrounds
  • Regent’s Park & Primrose Hill – views over the city skyline
  • St James’s Park – near Buckingham Palace, pelicans
  • Greenwich Park – observatory, Prime Meridian line, river views

Shopping Areas

  • Oxford Street & Regent Street – big brands & flagship stores
  • Covent Garden – boutiques, market, street performers
  • Westfield London / Westfield Stratford City – large malls
  • Camden Market – alternative fashion, food stalls, music
  • Spitalfields & Brick Lane – vintage, markets, street art

Emergency & Embassy Help

UK Emergency Numbers

  • 999 – Police, ambulance, fire (life-threatening emergencies)
  • 112 – EU-wide emergency number (also works in the UK)
  • 111 – Non-emergency medical advice (NHS)

When you call, state clearly: what happened, where you are, and if anyone is injured.

Personal SOS Plan

  1. Save local emergency numbers (999 / 112 / 111) in your phone.
  2. Keep your passport photo & travel insurance details in a safe place.
  3. Share your accommodation address & phone with family / friends.
  4. Use trusted taxi services or apps – avoid unlicensed minicabs.
